Subfloor preparation

Level floors before the finished floor goes down

Most flooring problems start below the visible surface. High spots, dips, old adhesive, uneven joins and moisture concerns can affect board movement, locking joins, acoustic underlay and the finished look.

Oz Timber Floor treats levelling as part of the finished flooring result, not a last-minute patch. The aim is to check the substrate, explain the practical preparation options and make the floor ready for the product being installed.

When it matters

How we approach it

Practical checks before compound

The right preparation depends on the substrate, existing floor, chosen product and how the space will be used.

Inspect the current surface

Look for high spots, low spots, cracks, residue, movement, moisture risk and old flooring issues.

Match prep to product

Hybrid, laminate, vinyl, engineered timber and solid timber each have different tolerance and installation needs.

Plan heights and edges

Door clearances, stairs, trims, skirting, ramps and adjoining rooms should be considered before levelling.

Prepare for installation

Once the substrate is suitable, product supply and installation can be planned with fewer surprises.
