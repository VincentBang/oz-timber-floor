Subfloor preparation

Level floors before the finished floor goes down

Most flooring problems start below the visible surface. High spots, dips, old adhesive, uneven joins and moisture concerns can affect board movement, locking systems, acoustic underlay and the finished look.

Oz Timber Floor treats levelling as part of the flooring system, not a last-minute patch. The aim is to check the substrate, explain the practical preparation options and make the floor ready for the product being installed.

Levelling work may include

  • Checking flatness across rooms, hallways and doorways before installation.
  • Preparing concrete slabs, timber sheet substrates and transition areas.
  • Using patching or self-levelling compound where the site and product system suit it.
  • Planning floor height, trims, expansion gaps, doors and adjoining surfaces before work begins.

How we approach it

Practical checks before compound

The right preparation depends on the substrate, existing floor, chosen product and how the space will be used.

Inspect the current surface

Look for high spots, low spots, cracks, residue, movement, moisture risk and old flooring issues.

Match prep to product

Hybrid, laminate, vinyl, engineered timber and solid timber each have different tolerance and installation needs.

Plan heights and edges

Door clearances, stairs, trims, skirting, ramps and adjoining rooms should be considered before levelling.

Prepare for installation

Once the substrate is suitable, product supply and installation can be planned with fewer surprises.
