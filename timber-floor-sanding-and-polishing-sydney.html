Restoration service

Restore the floor when the boards are worth saving

Existing timber floors can often be improved when the boards are thick enough, structurally sound and not too damaged by moisture, deep staining or past sanding.

Sanding and polishing should begin with a realistic assessment of board condition, coating failure, scratches, gaps, repairs and the finish customers expect after work is complete.

Restoration may include

  • Assessment of wear, scratches, coating failure, board condition and repair areas.
  • Sanding preparation before coating or polishing suitable timber floors.
  • Finish discussions for sheen, colour direction and maintenance expectations.
  • Advice when removal, replacement or partial repair is more practical than restoration.

What affects the finish

The finished result depends on the timber, past coatings, damage, repairs and site access as much as the sanding process itself.

Board condition

Thickness, cupping, movement, water damage and past sanding history influence whether restoration is suitable.

Existing coatings

Old coatings, stains and contaminants can affect sanding effort and final appearance.

Repairs and gaps

Damaged boards, missing sections and gaps should be discussed before finishing expectations are set.

Finish choice

Sheen, colour direction, drying time and maintenance expectations should match the way the space is used.
