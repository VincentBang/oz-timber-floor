Do you want something different instead of carpet tiles or tile flooring? Office flooring shapes first impressions and quietly absorbs the wear and tear of people, furniture and equipment moving across it every day. An office carpet will always need deep cleaning, so why not go for flooring easier to handle?

In a commercial environment, flooring needs to look professional for clients, feel comfortable for staff, and stand up to constant use without becoming a maintenance headache.

If you’re planning a new fit-out or upgrading an existing space, here’s how to choose flooring that works as hard as your business does.

What Matters Most in Office Flooring?

Every office operates differently, but there are a few core considerations that apply across the board.

How many people are in your office
Reception areas, corridors and open-plan workspaces experience far more traffic than meeting rooms or executive offices. Flooring needs to suit the busiest zones without looking worn.

The acoustics and how comfortable it is
Hard flooring doesn’t have to mean noisy. The right materials and underlay can reduce sound transfer.

The maintenance
In an office, downtime costs money. Flooring that is easy to clean, resistant to spills and forgiving of daily wear helps keep ongoing costs under control.

How the flooring is designed
Flooring plays a big role in how your business is perceived. From sleek and modern to warm and sophisticated, the right finish reinforces your brand identity.

Vinyl Flooring: Practical and Professional

Modern vinyl options closely replicate the appearance of timber and offers resistance to scratches, spills and heavy foot traffic. This makes it ideal for open-plan offices, shared workspaces and high-traffic zones.

Vinyl is also one of the easiest flooring options to maintain. Regular cleaning keeps it looking sharp, and its durability helps reduce the need for repairs or early replacement.

Best suited to: Busy offices, co-working spaces, and areas where durability and low maintenance are essential.

Bamboo Flooring: Sustainable with Style

Bamboo flooring is a sustainable option and also unique from traditional hardwoods and can become a subtle talking point with clients and visitors. Naturally hard-wearing, the floor handles moisture better than many people expect, so it is suitable for active office environments. 

Best suited to: Design-led offices, creative studios and businesses with a sustainability focus.

Laminate Flooring: Cost-Effective and Consistent

Laminate flooring delivers the visual appeal of timber at a more accessible price point. It’s engineered to resist scratches, stains and everyday wear.

Its consistent finish works well in larger spaces and its resistance to spills makes it practical for shared work areas.

Best suited to: Busy offices, administrative spaces and businesses managing fit-out budgets.

Hybrid Flooring: Built for Performance

Hybrid flooring combines the best qualities of laminate and vinyl into one. It is designed for high-traffic areas, resisting moisture and staying stable across large areas. From natural timber looks to more contemporary designs, hybrid flooring gives you freedom of choice.

Best suited to: Commercial offices seeking a strong visual statement that also lasts a long time.

View our Aspire Coastal Blackbutt hybrid floor option.

Engineered Timber: Natural Beauty

Engineered timber flooring offers the warmth and authenticity of real wood, but it doesn’t snap on impact.

Featuring a timber top layer over a layered core, engineered timber is less prone to movement than solid timber while still looking premium. It works beautifully in executive offices, boardrooms and reception areas where first impressions matter. 

Best suited to: Corporate offices, executive suites and spaces you want looking professional for clients.

Solid Timber: Timeless

Solid timber flooring remains the gold standard for businesses. It can be sanded and refinished multiple times, extending its lifespan well beyond many other flooring types.

While it represents a higher initial investment, solid timber delivers long-term value and sophistication in any office space.

Best suited to: High-end offices, professional services and long-term commercial investments.
