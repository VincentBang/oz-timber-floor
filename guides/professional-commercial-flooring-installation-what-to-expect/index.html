Full guide

Installing new flooring in a commercial space is not something you want rushed, improvised or treated as a one-size-fits-all job. Offices, retail spaces and other commercial environments place very different demands on flooring compared to residential projects, and the installation process matters just as much as the product itself.

If you are planning a commercial flooring project for your shop or office, here is what you can realistically expect when working with a professional team like Oz Timber Floor.

A Proper Site Inspection Before Anything Else

A professional commercial installation always starts on-site, not with assumptions.

Before recommending products or providing a final quote, the Oz Timber Floor team inspects your space in person. This helps with access to:

  • Subfloor condition and level
  • Moisture levels and environmental factors
  • Existing floor coverings and removal requirements
  • Traffic flow, furniture load and usage patterns
  • Access, timing and business disruption considerations

This step is critical in commercial environments, where subfloor preparation and moisture control can directly affect long-term performance. It also means the flooring solution will be suited to how your business actually operates, not just how the space looks on paper.

Solid Timber Flooring

For businesses seeking a timeless, high-end result, solid timber remains as a gold standard. Oz Timber Floor installs both raw Australian hardwoods and pre-finished solid timber, depending on the desired look and timeframe.

Gorgeous raw hardwood options include:

  • American White Oak
  • Cypress Pine
  • Grey Box

Pre-finished solid timber options include:

  • Brushbox
  • Jarrah
  • Kempas

Solid timber requires precise installation and careful preparation, which is why experience matters in commercial settings.

What about hybrid, vinyl, and laminate options?

For high-traffic areas or projects requiring faster installation and lower maintenance, Oz Timber Floor also installs hybrid, vinyl and laminate flooring.

These options work well for open-plan offices, retail environments and shared commercial spaces where you need the flooring to be strong.

Correct subfloor preparation

One of the biggest differences between a professional installation and a poor one is what happens beneath the surface.

Oz Timber Floor takes subfloor preparation seriously. Depending on the site, this may include levelling, moisture barriers or adjustments to ensure the flooring sits correctly and performs long-term. Skipping this step is one of the most common causes of premature flooring failure, particularly in commercial spaces.

Doing it properly the first time protects your investment and avoids repairs later. Learn more here: Laying the Foundations Right: Why Floor Levelling Matters for Your Timber Flooring.

Skilled installation

Commercial flooring installation requires precision, consistency and attention to detail, especially across large areas.

Oz Timber Floor’s installers are experienced across a wide range of commercial projects, from small offices to large-scale fit-outs. They understand expansion allowances, joins, transitions and finishing details that affect both appearance and durability.

The result is a floor that not only looks right on day one, but performs well every day.

Finishing, clean up and handover

Once installation is complete, the job is not simply packed up and left behind.

Oz Timber Floor ensures:

  • Finishes and trims are properly installed
  • The site is cleaned and ready for use
  • Care and maintenance advice is clearly explained

For timber floors, this includes guidance on cleaning, ongoing maintenance and how to protect the surface in high-use areas.

Ongoing support and maintenance

Commercial flooring does not stop at installation. Oz Timber Floor also offers services such as sanding, polishing and maintenance, allowing businesses to refresh or restore timber floors over time rather than replacing them earlier than intended.
