Why is Floor Levelling Essential?

Floor levelling isn’t just about fixing visible imperfections like cracks or stains on your property’s flooring. It’s about creating a solid and level base for your timber floors. If your underlying concrete slab isn’t flat and smooth, your timber floors could face issues down the road, which could cost you more.

Think of floor levelling as preparing and laying the groundwork for your timber floor to perform at its best. Without it, you risk structural issues, increased wear and tear, and reduced aesthetic appeal.

The Process of Floor Levelling

At its core, floor levelling tackles the uneven concrete subfloor underneath your main floor. This process identifies and evens out disparities, ensuring a smooth surface. Our team utilises concrete grinding to decrease the size of these disparities and then fills in the remaining gaps with levelling concrete. This diligent approach ensures a smooth base, ready for your timber floor installation.

Advantages of Opting for Floor Levelling

Affordability: Floor levelling can prevent ongoing damage to your property, potentially saving you money in the long run.

Durability: Properly levelled floors provide a strong base, preventing premature wear and tear on your timber floor.

Aesthetics: With a smooth and even base, your timber floor will look better and be free of potential warping or other issues.

Safety: Uneven floors can be a tripping hazard. Levelled floors reduce this risk, ensuring a safer space.
