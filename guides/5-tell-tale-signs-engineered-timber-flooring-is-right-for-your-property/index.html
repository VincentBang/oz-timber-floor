What is Engineered Timber Flooring?

Engineered timber flooring is a type of hardwood flooring that involves several layers of wood bonded together to create a stable and durable surface. Unlike traditional solid hardwood flooring, which is made from a single piece of wood, engineered timber flooring features a top layer of natural hardwood veneer adhered to a core of plywood or high-density fibreboards (HDF).

This multi-layered construction imparts significant advantages, such as greater stability and resistance to moisture and temperature fluctuations, making it suitable for use in a variety of settings, including basements and areas with high humidity. Because the top layer is genuine hardwood, engineered timber flooring offers the same luxurious look and feel of solid wood floors, but with enhanced practicality and often at a more economical price point.

Engineered Timber Flooring vs. Solid Timber Flooring

The burning question most property owners ask themselves when deciding on their timber flooring is whether engineered or solid timber is better for my space?

Solid timber flooring is a traditional type of hardwood flooring made of natural solid wood. The reason for its popularity stems from its ability to be sanded multiple times throughout its cycle. However, it is more sensitive to environmental conditions like humidity and temperature, requiring more preventive maintenance.

Best suited for ground-level installations and above, solid timber flooring is not recommended for areas with high moisture like bathrooms or below-grade spaces like basements.

See our solid timber flooring options.

Engineered timber flooring, on the other hand, is generally more stable and less susceptible to warping from moisture or temperature changes, making it suitable for areas like basements and bathrooms. It is usually pre-finished and may not be as long-lasting as solid timber floors when it comes to refinishing.

Additionally, it is cost-effective both in terms of material and installation costs. The use of non-hardwood layers reduces the overall cost while delivering a similar aesthetic. As a result, it is Ideal for below-grade installations and over concrete subfloors or radiant heating systems, thanks to its moisture-resistant qualities.

Key Indicators Engineered Timber Flooring is Perfect for You

1. You’re Desire Both Aesthetic Appeal and Practicality

Engineered timber flooring offers the best of both worlds—the classic, ambient look of wood and the practical benefits of modern engineering. Unlike traditional hardwood, engineered timber is designed for durability, ease of installation, and resistance to environmental factors.

So, if you’re looking for flooring that combines the timeless elegance of wood with modern-day advantages, engineered timber is your go-to option.

2. You Need a Cost-Effective Flooring Solution

Engineered timber is generally more affordable compared to solid hardwood flooring, making it a practical choice for property owners on a budget. You can save money without compromising on the quality or aesthetics all while improving your properties commercial value.

3. You Require Quick and Easy Installation

Time is of the essence, especially when you’re in the middle of property renovations or building from scratch. Engineered timber flooring doesn’t require time to adapt to a new climate, like solid wood, saving you 7-10 days right off the bat. Plus, it often comes pre-finished, eliminating the need for sanding or coating after installation. If you’re looking to move quickly without sacrificing on quality, engineered timber flooring is a winner.

4. You’re Installing in Moisture-Prone Areas

If you’re considering flooring for areas like kitchens and bathrooms, engineered timber flooring is a strong candidate. It’s less prone to contraction and expansion due to humidity, making it more stable in moisture-prone environments. Additionally, engineered floors are compatible with water-fed underfloor heating systems, so your feet can stay warm even in cooler climates.

5. You Care About Acoustic Quality and Health Benefits

Noise reduction is a crucial consideration, especially for multi-level apartments. Engineered timber flooring adheres to the Building Code of Australia’s guidelines on sound absorption between floors, making it a great choice for those who value peace and quiet. Also, unlike carpets, engineered timber doesn’t harbour dust or allergens, making it a healthier choice for those with allergies or asthma.

Laying the Foundations Right with Engineered Timber Flooring

Choosing the right flooring for your property is a critical decision that impacts not just the appearance but also the practicality and long-term value of your space.

Engineered timber flooring stands out as an excellent choice for a variety of reasons. Its stunning visual appeal, cost-effectiveness, easy installation, suitability for moisture-prone areas, and health benefits make it a versatile option for both residential and commercial properties.
