Common questions

These answers help prepare a useful enquiry before product selection or installation planning.

Engineered timber flooring uses a real timber veneer over a multi-layer core. It gives the look and feel of genuine timber flooring with construction chosen for stability, durability and easier installation across many Sydney homes, apartments and commercial interiors.

It depends on the site and the brief. Engineered timber is often chosen where board stability, apartment acoustics, subfloor conditions and installation flexibility matter, while solid timber still suits traditional hardwood projects where sanding cycles and long-term refinishing are part of the plan.

Many engineered timber ranges can suit floating or direct-stick installations, but the right method depends on the board construction, acoustic requirements, subfloor condition and manufacturer instructions.

Often yes. Premium engineered timber flooring should be installed over a suitable, flat and stable subfloor. Concrete grinding, patching or self-levelling compound may be needed before installation.
