What it is

What is engineered timber flooring?

Engineered timber flooring uses a real timber veneer over a multi-layer core, combining genuine timber appearance with construction chosen for stability, durability and lower-maintenance day-to-day living.

Real timber surface

Engineered timber floors are not printed timber-look boards. They use genuine timber on the visible surface, which is why they suit premium interiors where natural grain and warmth matter.

Multi-layer core

The core construction helps many engineered timber floors perform well in apartments, builder projects and commercial interiors where stability and installation flexibility matter.

Low-maintenance premium finish

Engineered timber is often selected when customers want real timber character with a cleaner maintenance profile than some traditional site-finished hardwood systems.

Compare timber flooring options

Engineered timber floor vs solid timber floor

The best timber flooring choice depends on the site, the product brief and how the finished floor will be used.

Engineered timber

Often chosen for apartments, premium homes and offices where stability, acoustic planning and installation flexibility are important.

Solid timber

Still valuable for traditional hardwood projects where sanding cycles, custom finishing and long-term refinishing are part of the plan.

Floating installation

Some engineered timber ranges suit floating installation where the product, substrate and acoustic brief allow it.

Direct-stick installation

Other projects call for direct-stick engineered timber flooring, especially where feel, acoustic performance and site conditions need a tighter specification.

Preparation matters

Subfloor preparation and floor levelling before engineered timber

Premium engineered timber should not go over an uneven or unstable surface. Oz checks subfloor flatness, moisture risks, old adhesive, transition heights and whether concrete grinding, patching or self-levelling compound is needed before installation.

That preparation work is especially important when engineered timber is being used in apartments, direct-stick installations, commercial interiors or builder projects with staged handover deadlines.

Before installation, confirm

  • Whether the range suits floating or direct-stick installation.
  • Concrete slab flatness, moisture risk and old floor residue.
  • Trim, staircase, skirting and expansion detail planning.
  • Access, strata rules, working hours and after-trade protection.
