Site checks

Common site details that affect flooring work

Helpful site details protect timing, finish quality and quoting accuracy before the work is booked.

Check

Floor flatness

Uneven areas can change the preparation scope before hybrid, laminate or timber installation.

Review

Moisture and substrate condition

Concrete slabs, repaired areas, sheet substrates and old adhesive should be checked early.

Plan

Existing flooring removal

Old carpet, timber, laminate or vinyl may need removal before the final floor can be quoted clearly.

Detail

Trims, transitions and skirting

Doorways, stairs, adjoining floors and skirting details affect the finished flooring result.

Access

Apartment access, lifts and parking

Occupied sites, strata buildings and city projects can need access planning before installation day.

Timing

Builder handover and site readiness

Other trades, staged areas and builder timing can change when flooring work should begin.

FAQ

Questions about flooring services

These answers help customers send a more useful enquiry before a site visit or product shortlist is finalised.

Which flooring service do I need?

It depends on whether you need product supply, installation, floor preparation, existing floor removal, sanding and polishing or commercial coordination.

Can I request supply and installation together?

Yes. Include suburb, approximate floor area, current floor type, preferred product and timing so Oz Timber Floor can review the next step.

Do I need floor levelling before installation?

Some floors need preparation before hybrid, laminate or timber flooring can be installed properly. Floor flatness and site condition should be reviewed early.

Can Oz Timber Floor help with apartments or builder projects?

Yes. Apartment, builder and commercial projects may need planning for access, lifts, timing, product suitability and staged work.

Can I send photos or a floor plan?

Yes. Photos, room sizes and floor plans can help Oz Timber Floor understand the project before confirming the right service path.
