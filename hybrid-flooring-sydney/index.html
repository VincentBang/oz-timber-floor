Product category

Low-maintenance flooring still needs a prepared base

Hybrid flooring is popular because it combines timber-look design with practical maintenance for busy homes, apartments, rentals and selected commercial spaces.

It is not a shortcut around preparation. The subfloor still needs to be suitable, acoustic requirements may apply in apartments, and transitions, skirting, door heights and product availability should be checked before installation.

Hybrid enquiries can cover

  • Supply-only pricing, stock checks and close alternative colours.
  • Supply + install planning for homes, apartments, rentals and light commercial spaces.
  • Floor levelling and subfloor preparation before floating floor installation.
  • Trims, underlay, acoustic requirements and staged work for managed sites.

Buying checks

What to confirm before ordering hybrid

Hybrid flooring performs best when product choice, subfloor readiness and installation details are handled together.

Flat and stable floor

Check slab or substrate flatness before installation to avoid movement, bounce and stressed joins.

Apartment requirements

Confirm acoustic underlay, strata approval, lift access and working-hour rules where relevant.

Room transitions

Plan door trims, expansion gaps, skirting, scotia and adjoining floor heights before ordering.

Stock and lead times

Ask about current availability and alternatives before locking in a colour across multiple rooms.
