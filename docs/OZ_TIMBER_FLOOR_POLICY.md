# Oz Timber Floor Project Policy

## Project Boundary

Oz Timber Floor is separate from Operon Flooring.

Oz Timber Floor is a traditional flooring supplier and contractor website with a catalogue and enquiry model. Codex must not modify Operon Flooring files, repositories, folders, components, product data, quote flows, floorplan tools, quote review features, pricing logic, API logic or backend systems.

Operon Flooring may only be inspected as a read-only visual and structural reference for product catalogue browsing patterns.

Codex must not copy Operon quote flow, pricing logic, floorplan tool, quote review, backend logic or API keys into Oz Timber Floor.

Codex must never modify Operon Flooring files or copy Operon quote, pricing, floorplan, quote review or backend logic into Oz Timber Floor. Operon may be inspected only as a design/reference source when explicitly requested.

## Catalogue And Enquiry Model

The Oz Timber Floor product catalogue is catalogue/enquiry only. The site must not include:

- Checkout.
- Instant pricing.
- Internal pricing exposure.
- Operon quote flow or quote review behaviour.
- Floorplan upload or floorplan quoting tools.

Product CTAs should lead to enquiries such as supply price requests, stock checks, supply + install enquiries and product questions.

## Supplier Data Rules

Supplier and brand names may appear on public catalogue pages only as verified customer-facing catalogue labels.

Supplier data must be checked against source pages before product details are imported or corrected. Do not guess product information, invent specifications, or silently change unusual supplier spellings without source confidence.

Unknown specifications should remain blank in data, with customer-facing pages using a confirmation note where useful.

## Image Rules

Product images must match the colour name and category.

Do not hotlink supplier images.

Images used on the Oz Timber Floor site must be local, compressed and traceable to a source page or source audit note.

Do not use generic, wrong-category or mismatched colour images to fill gaps. Use the approved image-coming-soon asset until an exact image is verified.

## Redirects And SEO

Do not remove existing redirects or SEO-preserved pages.

When slugs are corrected, keep old URLs working with redirects unless Vincent approves a different migration plan.
